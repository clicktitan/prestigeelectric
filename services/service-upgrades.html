Signs you need an upgrade

When your home needs a service upgrade

These are the most common reasons Kansas City homeowners call about upgrading electrical service. If several apply at once, your system may be undersized for how you use power today.

Frequent breaker trips under normal use

Breakers that trip when the dryer, HVAC, or kitchen appliances run together often mean the service or panel cannot handle combined load.

Planning a major addition or remodel

Finished basements, garage workshops, and room additions usually add circuits. An upgrade ensures the service entrance can support the new demand.

Adding a hot tub, EV charger, or large appliance

High-draw equipment often requires dedicated circuits and sufficient overall capacity. Upgrading first prevents repeated tripping and unsafe workarounds.

Flickering lights when appliances start

Voltage drop when motors and compressors kick on can signal an undersized service, loose connections, or an aging panel struggling to keep up.

An older 60 or 100 amp service

Many Kansas City metro homes still run on legacy service sizes that were fine decades ago but fall short for modern kitchens, laundry, and HVAC.

No open spaces left in the panel

When every breaker slot is used, adding circuits without upgrading can lead to unsafe tandem breakers or overloaded bus bars.

Insurance or inspection requirements

Some real estate transactions, insurance renewals, or permit inspections flag outdated service equipment that should be brought up to current standards.

Utility meter or mast damage

Weather, age, or prior repairs to the service entrance may require coordinated utility and electrician work to restore safe, code-compliant power.

Service Upgrades questions

How much does an electrical service upgrade cost in Kansas City?

Cost depends on your current service size, panel condition, utility requirements, and the target amperage. Prestige Electric provides upfront flat-rate pricing after an on-site evaluation so you know what to expect before work begins.

When do I need an electrical service upgrade?

Common signs include frequent breaker trips, flickering lights when appliances run, an outdated panel, no open breaker spaces, or plans for major additions like hot tubs, EV chargers, or workshop equipment.

What is the difference between a service upgrade and a panel replacement?

A service upgrade increases the capacity coming into the property, often including the meter base and service entrance. A panel replacement focuses on the distribution panel inside. Prestige Electric will recommend the right combination after reviewing your system.

Do you coordinate with the utility company?

Yes. Service upgrades often require utility disconnect and reconnect. Prestige Electric handles the electrical scope and coordinates timing so the upgrade is completed safely and to code.
