Signs you need an upgrade

When to upgrade your breaker box

These signs suggest your breaker box may not meet today's safety standards or your home's electrical demand. A licensed electrician can confirm whether an upgrade is the right step.

No open breaker spaces

When every slot is occupied, adding circuits for hot tubs, EV chargers, or basement finishes requires a larger or replacement panel.

Outdated or recalled panel brands

Certain legacy panels and breakers are known reliability concerns. Upgrading to modern equipment reduces long-term risk.

Breakers that will not reset

A breaker that trips instantly or feels loose may have failed internally and should be evaluated as part of the panel.

Double-tapped or overcrowded wiring

Improper connections inside the panel are a fire hazard. An upgrade or reorganization may be needed to meet code.

Adding major new loads

Workshops, tankless water heaters, and spa circuits often require both new breakers and a panel that can support them safely.

Rust, scorch marks, or burning smell

Visible damage or odor at the panel is an urgent sign that the breaker box should be inspected and likely upgraded.

Frequent nuisance tripping

If standard breakers trip often, the issue may be undersized circuits, failing breakers, or a panel that needs modern AFCI or GFCI protection.

Home inspection flagged the panel

Real estate and insurance inspections sometimes identify panels that should be upgraded before sale or policy renewal.

FAQ

Breaker Box Upgrade questions

How much does a breaker box upgrade cost in Kansas City?

Cost depends on panel size, the number of circuits, breaker types required, and whether the service entrance also needs work. Prestige Electric provides upfront flat-rate pricing after an on-site evaluation.

When should I upgrade my breaker box?

Consider an upgrade if your panel is outdated, you lack open breaker spaces, breakers trip frequently, or you are adding major loads like hot tubs, EV chargers, or workshop equipment.

Is a breaker box upgrade different from a panel replacement?

They are related. A breaker box upgrade focuses on improving capacity, safety, and modern breaker protection. A full panel replacement may be needed when the enclosure or bus is failing. Prestige Electric will recommend the right solution after reviewing your system.

Can you add AFCI or GFCI breakers during an upgrade?

Yes. When upgrading, we can install the appropriate combination of standard, AFCI, and GFCI breakers to meet current code and protect specific circuits.
