Signs you need a new panel

When to replace your electrical panel

These warning signs appear in Kansas City homes with aging or overloaded panels. If you notice any of them, schedule a licensed electrician before the problem worsens.

Buzzing or crackling from the panel

Unusual sounds from the breaker box often indicate loose bus connections, failing breakers, or arcing inside the enclosure.

Warm breakers or a hot panel cover

Heat at the panel means connections or breakers are under stress. This is a safety concern that should be inspected promptly.

Corrosion or rust on the panel

Moisture intrusion or age can corrode panel components, reducing reliability and increasing fire risk.

Frequent breaker trips

Breakers that trip repeatedly may be doing their job on overloaded circuits, or the panel itself may no longer handle your home's demand.

Federal Pacific, Zinsco, or other recalled brands

Certain legacy panel brands are known safety concerns. Replacement is often the recommended path for long-term peace of mind.

No room for new circuits

When every slot is full, adding hot tubs, EV chargers, or basement circuits may require a larger panel with open capacity.

Flickering lights across multiple rooms

Widespread dimming or flickering can trace back to a failing main connection or deteriorating panel components.

Home built before modern code standards

Older Kansas City metro homes may still have panels that never accounted for today's kitchen, laundry, and HVAC loads.
